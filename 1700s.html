George Washington

George Washington was an American soldier, statesman, and Founding Father who served as the first president of the United States from 1789 to 1797. He led the American colonial army to victory in the American Revolution and played a crucial role in the drafting of the U.S. Constitution.

Washington is revered as the president who set the standard for those who followed him, by serving the nation with dignity and intelligence. He is often called the "Father of His Country" for his pivotal role in the founding of the United States.

Jasly Delmonte, 2024
